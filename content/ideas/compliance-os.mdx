## The Problem: Compliance Is Broken

Every company operating in a regulated industry — fintech, healthtech, cannabis, crypto, insurance — faces the same brutal reality: compliance is manual, fragmented, and absurdly expensive. A mid-size fintech startup can easily spend $500K–$2M annually on compliance, much of it going to consultants shuffling spreadsheets and PDFs between teams.

The global regulatory compliance market is valued at **$50 billion** and growing at 12% CAGR. Yet the tooling looks like it was built in 2005. Most companies cobble together a Frankenstein stack of shared drives, ticketing systems, and expensive GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) platforms that require six-month implementations and six-figure contracts.

**ComplianceOS** is the opportunity to build the operating system that replaces all of it.

### Why Now?

Three converging forces make this the right moment:

1. **Regulatory acceleration**: The EU AI Act, SEC crypto rules, and evolving US state privacy laws (beyond CCPA) are creating a tsunami of new requirements. Companies cannot hire fast enough.
2. **AI maturity**: Large language models can now parse regulatory text, map it to internal controls, and flag gaps — tasks that previously required expensive legal counsel.
3. **Compliance-as-code movement**: DevOps teams are already adopting infrastructure-as-code. The next wave is policy-as-code, where compliance rules are version-controlled, testable, and automated.

## The Product

### Core Features

**1. Regulation Engine**
A structured database of regulations — federal, state, and international — that maps requirements to specific controls. When a new regulation drops, the engine parses it and alerts affected customers with a clear action plan.

**2. Control Mapping & Gap Analysis**
Upload your existing policies and the AI maps them against applicable regulations, highlighting gaps. Instead of hiring a $400/hr consultant to review your SOC 2 readiness, the platform does it in minutes.

**3. Evidence Collection**
Automated integrations with AWS, GCP, Azure, GitHub, Jira, Slack, and HR systems to continuously collect compliance evidence. No more quarterly fire drills to gather screenshots.

**4. Policy-as-Code**
Write compliance policies in a structured format (YAML/JSON) that can be version-controlled, tested in CI/CD, and automatically validated against regulatory requirements.

**5. Audit Dashboard**
When auditors arrive, hand them a single link. Real-time dashboard with all evidence, controls, and attestations organized by framework. Reduce audit prep from weeks to hours.

### Technical Architecture

- **Backend**: Node.js / Python microservices on Kubernetes
- **Regulation Parser**: Fine-tuned LLM pipeline for extracting structured requirements from legal text
- **Integrations Layer**: OAuth-based connectors to 50+ SaaS tools and cloud providers
- **Frontend**: React dashboard with role-based access (compliance officer, engineering, auditor)
- **Storage**: PostgreSQL for structured data, S3 for evidence artifacts, vector DB for semantic regulation search
