## The Problem: The Recruiting Gap for Growing Companies

There's a massive gap in the recruiting software market between two tiers:

**Enterprise (150+ employees)**: Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, iCIMS. Sophisticated, feature-complete platforms with structured interviewing, offer management, DE&I analytics, and HRIS integration. Price: $15,000–$50,000+/year. Requires a dedicated recruiter or HR team to operate.

**SMB (0–10 employees)**: Workable, BreezyHR, JazzHR. Basic ATS functionality — job posting, application tracking, basic email templates. Price: $149–$299/month. No AI, no sourcing, no intelligence.

**The missing middle**: Companies with 10–150 employees. There are approximately **600,000 companies** in this size range in the US. They make 3–10 hires per year. They don't have a dedicated recruiting team (hiring is the CEO or a department manager's part-time responsibility). They need the capabilities of Greenhouse but can't justify the price or complexity.

### What "Recruiting" Actually Looks Like at a 40-Person Company

When a growing SMB needs to hire, this is what happens:

1. **The hiring manager writes a job description** — usually a copy-paste from a similar job they found on LinkedIn, with requirements that don't actually match what they need
2. **They post to Indeed and LinkedIn** — and get flooded with applicants of wildly varying quality, many of whom applied via one-click apply without reading the description
3. **Resume review** — 2–4 hours of manual reading, filtering by gut feel with no consistency or documentation
4. **Screening calls** — 30-minute calls that are often redundant (the information is on the resume) and poorly structured
5. **Scheduling coordination** — 8–12 emails to schedule each interview, often taking 3–5 days
6. **Interview debriefs** — informal conversations with no structured scoring, leading to "I liked her" vs. "I preferred the other guy" decisions
7. **Reference checks** — often skipped because they're time-consuming and "nobody gives a bad reference anyway"
8. **Offer management** — a Word document, negotiations by phone, an offer letter sent as a PDF

Total time investment per hire: **40–80 hours** of hiring manager time. At a manager's loaded cost of $80/hour, that's **$3,200–$6,400 in internal labor cost** per hire — before any job board fees, agency fees, or failed-hire costs.

### The Cost of Getting It Wrong

The real problem isn't the time — it's the **failure rate**. Studies consistently show that 46% of new hires fail within 18 months, primarily because:
- Skills were misassessed in the interview process
- Cultural fit wasn't evaluated rigorously
- The job description didn't accurately describe the role
- References weren't checked (or the right questions weren't asked)

A bad hire at the $80K salary level costs the company **$16,000–$32,000** in direct costs (severance, re-hiring, training) plus the productivity loss and team disruption.

SMBs fail at hiring not because they make lazy decisions — they make rushed decisions with too little data and too little structure.

## The Idea: AI-Powered Recruiting Automation for the Mid-Market

**HireLoop** is an AI recruiting platform built specifically for companies with 10–150 employees. It handles the four most time-intensive parts of recruiting — screening, sourcing, scheduling, and structure — making it possible for a busy hiring manager to run a rigorous hiring process in 8 hours instead of 80.

### Module 1 — Intelligent Application Screening

When candidates apply, HireLoop doesn't just store their resumes — it evaluates them.

**AI resume scoring**: Each application is scored on a 0–100 scale against the role's requirements. The model considers:
- Required skills match (hard requirements from the job description)
- Experience relevance (similar roles, similar-stage companies, relevant industries)
- Career trajectory (ascending responsibility, appropriate tenure patterns)
- Education fit (when relevant)
- "Must avoid" flags (job-hopping patterns beyond threshold, employment gaps without explanation, missing hard requirements)

**Knockout questions**: HireLoop automatically generates 3–5 role-specific screening questions and sends them to every applicant after they apply. Answers are AI-scored. A question like "Describe your experience with distributed database architecture" generates a free-text answer that HireLoop scores for depth, accuracy, and relevance — distinguishing the senior engineer from the candidate who listed "PostgreSQL" as a skill on a resume but has never worked with databases at scale.

**Stack ranking with explanation**: The hiring manager opens HireLoop and sees candidates ranked, with a plain-language explanation for each score: "Sarah ranked #3 of 127 applicants. Strong match: 6 years backend engineering with 3 at a similar-stage SaaS company, strong system design background, her knockout answer demonstrated deep knowledge of database indexing. Minor gap: no direct experience with your specific cloud provider (AWS vs. her GCP background), but this is learnable."

**Time saved**: From 4 hours of resume review to 45 minutes reviewing HireLoop's ranked stack with explanations.

### Module 2 — Proactive Sourcing

For roles where inbound applications are insufficient (senior engineering, specialized skills, executive), HireLoop runs outbound sourcing campaigns.

**Candidate discovery**: HireLoop searches LinkedIn, GitHub, AngelList, and HireLoop's own candidate network for people matching the role criteria. AI filters for passive candidates — people currently employed at relevant companies who haven't applied for jobs in 6+ months (signal: they're not desperate, which usually means they're good).

**Personalized outreach generation**: For each candidate, HireLoop generates a personalized cold outreach message based on their actual background: "Hi Marcus — I noticed your work on the Kafka consumer group optimization at TechCorp that you published on Medium. We're building similar infrastructure at [Company] and could use someone with your distributed systems depth..."

This personalization isn't a mail-merge — it pulls specific details from the candidate's profile and writes genuinely personalized messages. Response rates are 3–4× higher than generic outreach.

**Outreach management**: Automated follow-up sequences (2 touches, then stop), response tracking, and candidate pipeline management. The hiring manager approves the message templates and candidate list, HireLoop handles execution.

### Module 3 — Automated Scheduling

The biggest time drain in recruiting isn't decision-making — it's **logistics**.

HireLoop integrates with Google Calendar and Outlook to read the interviewing team's availability, then:
1. Sends candidates a self-scheduling link with available slots
2. Confirms the interview with a calendar invite to all parties
3. Sends automated reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before
4. Handles rescheduling requests without human involvement

For a typical 3-stage interview process, this eliminates **15–25 scheduling emails per candidate**.

### Module 4 — Structured Interviews and Collaborative Decisions

The highest-leverage intervention in recruiting is **structured interviewing** — asking every candidate the same questions with the same scoring rubric. Research shows structured interviews are 2× more predictive of job performance than unstructured conversations.

**Automated interview guides**: Based on the role and level, HireLoop generates a complete structured interview guide with behavioral questions, technical questions, scoring rubrics, and suggested follow-up probes. Interviewers get this guide 24 hours before the interview.

**Scorecard collection**: After each interview, the interviewer receives a mobile-friendly scorecard with the structured questions and a 1–5 rating scale. Takes 8 minutes to complete. HireLoop aggregates scores across all interviewers and shows where there was consensus and where there was disagreement.

**Decision meeting prep**: HireLoop generates a candidate comparison summary: side-by-side scorecard averages, interviewer notes, compensation expectations, and a recommendation based on the aggregated scores. The hiring team spends 20 minutes making a data-informed decision instead of 90 minutes of "well, I liked..." conversations.
