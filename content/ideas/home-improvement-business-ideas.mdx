The US home improvement market exceeded **$500 billion in 2025** and continues to grow as homeowners invest in their properties amid rising housing costs, remote work adoption, and an aging housing stock. From essential repairs to luxury renovations, skilled trades and home services professionals are in high demand — and often short supply.

What makes home improvement so attractive for entrepreneurs: customers come to you with an urgent, often non-discretionary need, profit margins for skilled labor are high, and strong craftsmanship builds a referral network that can sustain a business for decades. You don't need expensive equipment or a storefront to start most home improvement businesses.

This guide covers **10 home improvement business ideas** that are high-demand, profitable, and well-positioned for 2026.

---

## Why Home Improvement Is a Strong Business Sector

- **Non-discretionary demand** — roofs leak, pipes break, and HVAC systems fail regardless of economic conditions
- **Aging housing stock** — the median US home is 42 years old, creating massive repair and renovation demand
- **Remote work tailwind** — millions of homeowners upgraded their living spaces and continue doing so
- **Skilled labor shortage** — demand for qualified tradespeople consistently outpaces supply
- **High referral rate** — quality work in a neighborhood generates organic referrals to neighbors and friends
- **Recurring revenue potential** — maintenance contracts, seasonal services, and inspections create predictable income

## 1. Handyman Service

**Startup cost:** $2,000–$10,000

**Income potential:** $60,000–$200,000+/year

**What it is:** Provide general home repairs and maintenance — mounting TVs, fixing doors, patching drywall, assembling furniture, minor plumbing and electrical fixes, caulking, and a wide range of small jobs that homeowners lack the time, tools, or skill to tackle themselves.

**Why it works:** There is infinite demand for a reliable, skilled handyman. The biggest competitive advantage in this business is simply showing up on time, communicating well, and doing quality work — traits in short supply in the trades. Most handymen are fully booked through referrals within 6–12 months.

**Unit economics:**
- Hourly rate: $60–$120/hour
- Daily capacity: 2–3 jobs at $300–$600 each
- 250 working days = $150,000–$450,000 gross before materials

## 2. Interior Design and Decorating

**Startup cost:** $2,000–$15,000

**Income potential:** $80,000–$500,000+/year

**What it is:** Help homeowners, real estate developers, and businesses create beautiful, functional spaces through furniture selection, color palettes, layout planning, and procurement. Interior designers may charge by the hour, flat project fee, or a percentage of total project budget. Interior decorating (no structural changes) requires no license in most states.

**Why it works:** The interior design market exceeded $150 billion globally in 2025. As homebuyers invest more in personalizing their spaces, demand for design expertise has never been higher. Virtual interior design platforms have made it accessible to work with clients nationwide, dramatically expanding your potential market beyond your local area.

**Service tiers:**
- E-design (virtual): $500–$2,000 per room
- In-home consultation: $150–$350/hour
- Full-service project management: 10–20% of total project budget

## 3. Landscaping and Lawn Care

**Startup cost:** $5,000–$30,000

**Income potential:** $80,000–$600,000+/year

**What it is:** Provide lawn mowing, edging, fertilizing, mulching, seasonal cleanups, and landscape design and installation services to residential and commercial clients. Many landscaping businesses offer tiered service packages, from basic lawn maintenance to full landscape design and hardscaping.

**Why it works:** Curb appeal drives property values, and most homeowners don't have the time or equipment to maintain their lawns properly. Once you acquire a customer, the relationship is recurring — weekly or bi-weekly cuts, seasonal cleanups, and annual mulching become automatic revenue. A crew of 3 managing 15–20 residential accounts per day generates $5,000–$10,000/week.

**Keys to success:**
- Route density matters — cluster clients geographically to minimize drive time
- Offer annual maintenance contracts for predictable recurring revenue
- Commercial accounts (HOAs, office parks, retail) provide consistent volume

## 4. Painting Contractor

**Startup cost:** $3,000–$15,000

**Income potential:** $70,000–$400,000+/year

**What it is:** Provide interior and exterior painting services for residential and commercial clients. From fresh coats on rental properties to premium color consultations for new homeowners, painting is one of the most accessible skilled trades to enter and scale.

**Why it works:** Paint is the highest-ROI home improvement project — a $3,000 exterior paint job adds $10,000+ to a home's perceived value. Painting contractors earn $35–$75/hour on labor while materials cost 10–20% of the job. Scaling from solo operator to a crew of 4–6 painters multiplies revenue without proportionally increasing your time investment.

**Unit economics:**
- Interior room repaint: $500–$1,200 per room
- Exterior house paint: $3,000–$8,000
- Commercial repaint contract: $10,000–$50,000+

## 5. Smart Home Installation

**Startup cost:** $5,000–$25,000

**Income potential:** $80,000–$350,000+/year

**What it is:** Install, configure, and integrate smart home systems — smart lighting, thermostats, security cameras, doorbells, locks, audio/video systems, and full home automation platforms (Control4, Lutron, Savant). Serve both new construction and retrofit residential clients.

**Why it works:** The global smart home market is expected to reach $338 billion by 2030. Many homeowners want smart technology but lack the technical expertise to install and integrate it properly. Professional installers who can deliver seamless, beautiful smart home experiences command premium rates and generate strong referrals in affluent neighborhoods.

**Service packages:**
- Basic smart home setup (thermostat, doorbell, camera): $500–$1,500
- Full home automation consultation + install: $5,000–$50,000+
- Ongoing support/monitoring retainer: $100–$300/month

## 6. Kitchen and Bathroom Remodeling

**Startup cost:** $15,000–$50,000

**Income potential:** $200,000–$2,000,000+/year

**What it is:** Manage and execute kitchen and bathroom renovation projects — from design through demolition, installation, and finishing. Remodeling contractors coordinate subcontractors (plumbers, electricians, tile setters) and manage the project timeline and budget for homeowners.

**Why it works:** Kitchen and bathroom remodels consistently rank as the top ROI home improvement projects. The average kitchen remodel runs $30,000–$80,000, with contractors earning 20–40% gross margin on materials plus labor. A small remodeling firm completing 12–15 projects per year can generate $1M+ in annual revenue.

**Keys to success:**
- Build a trusted network of licensed subcontractors
- Use project management software to stay on schedule and under budget
- Before/after photography builds a powerful portfolio for marketing

## 7. HVAC Services

**Startup cost:** $20,000–$60,000

**Income potential:** $100,000–$800,000+/year

**What it is:** Provide heating, ventilation, and air conditioning installation, maintenance, and repair services. HVAC technicians require EPA 608 certification for refrigerant handling and typically complete an apprenticeship or technical school program.

**Why it works:** HVAC is one of the highest-demand skilled trades, and a nationwide shortage of technicians drives wages and rates to premium levels. Emergency HVAC repairs in extreme weather (a broken AC in August) command $200–$400/hour — customers will pay anything to restore comfort. Maintenance contracts create predictable recurring revenue on top of repair work.

**Revenue model:**
- Seasonal maintenance visit: $150–$350/unit
- Emergency repair: $200–$500/hour
- Full system replacement: $5,000–$20,000

## 8. Cleaning Business (Residential)

**Startup cost:** $1,000–$5,000

**Income potential:** $60,000–$300,000+/year

**What it is:** Provide recurring residential cleaning services — standard cleaning, deep cleaning, move-in/move-out cleaning, and post-construction cleaning — to homeowners and renters. Many cleaning businesses operate as a sole proprietor before hiring a team and scaling to multiple crews.

**Why it works:** Cleaning is the most accessible home services business to start — low equipment cost, no licensing required in most states, and immediate demand from dual-income households. A residential cleaning business with 30 recurring weekly clients at $150/visit generates $23,400/month — and cleaning businesses with multiple crews can scale to 7 figures.

**Unit economics:**
- Standard 3-bedroom clean: $120–$200
- Deep clean: $200–$400
- 5 cleans/day at $150 average = $195,000/year gross (solo operator)

## 9. Roofing Contractor

**Startup cost:** $15,000–$50,000

**Income potential:** $150,000–$1,500,000+/year

**What it is:** Provide roof installation, repair, and replacement services for residential and commercial properties. Roofers handle shingle replacement, metal roofing, flat roofs, gutters, and storm damage restoration — often working with homeowner insurance claims.

**Why it works:** Roofing is a high-ticket, non-discretionary service. A full roof replacement averages $10,000–$25,000, and a small roofing company completing 5–8 jobs per week with a 2–3 crew generates $3M+ annually at scale. Storm chasers (contractors who follow hail and wind damage events) can do in one season what other contractors earn in a full year.

**Keys to success:**
- Build relationships with insurance adjusters for storm damage restoration work
- Safety systems and proper insurance are non-negotiable
- Invest in sales training — roofing is a high-consideration purchase

## 10. Deck and Fence Building

**Startup cost:** $10,000–$30,000

**Income potential:** $100,000–$600,000+/year

**What it is:** Design and build custom decks, pergolas, fences, and outdoor living structures for residential clients. Projects range from basic pressure-treated wood decks to premium composite decking systems with built-in lighting, outdoor kitchens, and fire pits.

**Why it works:** Outdoor living has exploded post-pandemic. Homeowners continue investing heavily in backyard spaces, and a well-built deck or outdoor structure dramatically increases livable square footage and property value. A custom composite deck runs $15,000–$60,000 — with skilled carpenter labor and quality materials, gross margins of 35–50% are achievable.

**Revenue benchmarks:**
- Basic wood deck (16x12): $8,000–$15,000
- Premium composite deck with pergola: $25,000–$60,000
- Full outdoor kitchen + living area: $40,000–$150,000

## How to Start a Home Improvement Business in 5 Steps

### Step 1: Choose Your Trade and Get Certified

Select one core service and develop genuine expertise. Many trades require licensing (contractors, electricians, plumbers, HVAC) — research your state's requirements and get certified before operating. Licensing protects you legally and signals credibility to customers.

### Step 2: Get Insured

General liability insurance ($1M–$2M coverage) is non-negotiable before your first job. Workers' compensation is required if you have employees. Many homeowners will ask for your insurance certificate before hiring — not having it costs you jobs.

### Step 3: Build Your Initial Customer Base

Start with your personal network, neighbors, and local Facebook groups. Offer a small number of discounted or free jobs to build reviews and before/after photos. Nextdoor, Google Business Profile, and Yelp are the highest-ROI platforms for home services in the first 12 months.

### Step 4: Build Systems for Quality and Reliability

Most home improvement businesses fail because of inconsistent quality and communication — not lack of demand. Invest in a simple CRM (Jobber, Housecall Pro) to manage estimates, scheduling, invoicing, and follow-ups. Systematized communication makes you stand out.

### Step 5: Scale with Hiring and Marketing

Once you have consistent work, hire your first helper or subcontractor. Train them to your quality standard. Redirect your time to sales, customer relationships, and finding the next job. Invest in Google Local Services Ads, which drive high-intent leads for most home improvement categories.
