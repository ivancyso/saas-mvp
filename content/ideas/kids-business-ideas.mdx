Teaching kids to run a business is one of the best gifts a parent or educator can give. Beyond earning spending money, young entrepreneurs develop **negotiation, financial literacy, problem-solving, and resilience** — skills that compound for life.

This guide covers **35 business ideas for kids** in 2026, organized by age, season, and type. All are age-appropriate, low-cost, and genuinely profitable.

---

## Why Kids Should Start Businesses Young

Children who start businesses early develop:

- **Financial literacy** — understanding profit, expenses, and reinvestment
- **Work ethic** — the connection between effort and reward
- **Communication skills** — pitching ideas, negotiating prices, satisfying customers
- **Resilience** — handling rejection and pivoting when plans don't work
- **Confidence** — accomplishing something real in the world

And the practical benefits are real: many successful adult entrepreneurs started as kids selling lemonade, lawn care, or handmade goods.

## Outdoor & Service Businesses (Ages 8–14)

These businesses are perfect for active kids who want to work in their neighborhood.

### 1. Lemonade Stand (Classic + Upgraded)

**Startup cost:** $10–$50

**Income potential:** $50–$500/day (event placement)

**What it is:** The classic entrepreneurship introduction — but modern versions go beyond the driveway. Position at community events, farmers markets, or sporting events for dramatically higher traffic.

**Why it works:** Lemonade stands teach product pricing, customer service, and supply chain basics. Upgraded versions with custom flavors or gourmet lemonade can charge $5–$7 per cup.

**How to start:** Make 3 varieties (classic, strawberry, mint), invest in a good sign, and position at a Saturday farmers market or youth sports event. One busy day can bring in $200–$500.

---

### 2. Lawn Mowing Service

**Startup cost:** $0–$200 (use parents' mower to start)

**Income potential:** $500–$2,000/month (summer)

**What it is:** Residential lawn mowing and basic yard maintenance in the neighborhood.

**Why it works:** Neighbors will pay $25–$50 per mow. Mow 3 yards on a Saturday morning and earn $75–$150 in 3 hours. Build a recurring route of 10–15 weekly clients over summer.

**Business skills learned:** Scheduling, reliability, client communication, physical work ethic.

---

### 3. Dog Walking & Pet Sitting

**Startup cost:** $0–$50 (leashes, waste bags)

**Income potential:** $300–$1,500/month

**What it is:** Walk dogs for busy neighbors or pet-sit during vacations and weekends.

**Why it works:** Pet owners pay $15–$30 per walk and $25–$60/night for pet sitting. Kids who love animals can build a loyal client base through Nextdoor and word of mouth.

**Execution tip:** Rover and Wag allow teens (with parent accounts) to list services. Start with neighbors for trust-building, then expand.

---

### 4. Car Washing

**Startup cost:** $20–$80 (bucket, soap, microfiber cloths, wax)

**Income potential:** $200–$600 on a good weekend

**What it is:** Offer hand car washes in driveways, at church parking lots, or at community events.

**Why it works:** A $20 hand wash is cheaper than a car wash drive-through and more personal. A 3-kid team can wash 10 cars in an afternoon. Charge $25–$40 with an interior wipe-down.

---

### 5. Snow Removal Service (Seasonal)

**Startup cost:** $20–$80 (shovel, ice melt)

**Income potential:** $100–$500 per storm

**What it is:** Shovel driveways and walkways after snowstorms for elderly neighbors and busy families.

**Why it works:** After a major storm, elderly homeowners will pay $30–$75 per driveway. A team of 2–3 kids can service 10–15 houses in a morning. Recurring contracts for the season generate reliable income.

---

### 6. Leaf Raking & Yard Cleanup

**Startup cost:** $10–$40 (rakes, bags)

**Income potential:** $100–$400/weekend (fall)

**What it is:** Fall leaf cleanup for residential properties — rake, bag, and haul.

**Why it works:** Many homeowners hate this chore. Charge $30–$80 per yard depending on size. Fall weekends in leaf-heavy neighborhoods can generate significant income in just a few hours.

---

### 7. Gardening Assistant

**Startup cost:** $0–$30 (basic tools)

**Income potential:** $200–$800/month (spring–fall)

**What it is:** Help elderly or busy neighbors with planting, weeding, watering, and general garden maintenance.

**Why it works:** Gardeners pay $15–$25/hour for helpers. Kids who learn plant care, soil prep, and watering schedules develop skills that translate into adult landscaping or agriculture careers.

## Crafts & Creative Businesses (Ages 8–16)

These businesses leverage kids' natural creativity and can be sold online or locally.

### 8. Handmade Jewelry & Accessories

**Startup cost:** $30–$100 (beads, wire, tools)

**Income potential:** $200–$2,000+/month

**What it is:** Create and sell handmade bracelets, necklaces, earrings, and hair accessories.

**Why it works:** Materials cost pennies; finished pieces sell for $5–$30+. Sell at school, craft fairs, or Etsy with a parent account. Instagram posts of new designs drive organic discovery.

---

### 9. Candle Making

**Startup cost:** $50–$150 (wax, wicks, fragrance, containers)

**Income potential:** $500–$3,000/month

**What it is:** Hand-pour scented soy candles and sell locally or online.

**Why it works:** A $3 candle in materials sells for $15–$25. Home goods markets and Etsy are flooded with candle buyers. Unique scent combinations and attractive packaging create differentiation.

---

### 10. Baked Goods Business

**Startup cost:** $20–$80 (ingredients, packaging)

**Income potential:** $100–$1,000/month

**What it is:** Bake cookies, cupcakes, brownies, or specialty breads and sell to neighbors, at school events, or online (check local cottage food laws).

**Why it works:** A batch of 24 cookies costs $3–$5 to make and sells for $20–$40. Specialty items (gluten-free, custom decorated) command premium prices.

**Important note:** Most states have cottage food laws that allow selling homemade baked goods without a commercial kitchen license. Research your local laws before selling.

---

### 11. Greeting Card & Stationery Design

**Startup cost:** $10–$50 (art supplies or printer)

**Income potential:** $100–$500/month

**What it is:** Design and sell handmade greeting cards, bookmarks, or stationery sets.

**Why it works:** Handmade cards sell for $4–$10 each. Artistically talented kids can produce dozens per week. Etsy, local gift shops, and craft fairs are natural sales channels.

---

### 12. Custom T-Shirt Printing

**Startup cost:** $100–$300 (iron-on transfers and plain shirts, or design + Printful)

**Income potential:** $300–$2,000+/month

**What it is:** Design custom t-shirts for sports teams, family reunions, school clubs, and events.

**Why it works:** Bulk orders for teams and events are highly profitable. A 20-shirt order at $20/shirt = $400. Using print-on-demand services, there's no inventory risk.

---

### 13. Face Painting

**Startup cost:** $30–$100 (professional face paints, brushes)

**Income potential:** $100–$400/event

**What it is:** Face painting at birthday parties, community events, and school fairs.

**Why it works:** Face painters at events charge $75–$200/hour. A skilled kid artist can build a reputation at local events and get booked regularly on weekends.

## Tech & Digital Businesses (Ages 12–18)

Older kids with tech skills can build surprisingly serious businesses.

### 14. Social Media Management

**Startup cost:** $0

**Income potential:** $200–$1,500/month

**What it is:** Manage Instagram, TikTok, or Facebook accounts for small local businesses that don't have time.

**Why it works:** Young people intuitively understand social media in ways adults often don't. Local restaurants, boutiques, and service businesses will pay $200–$600/month for consistent posting and engagement.

---

### 15. Tutoring

**Startup cost:** $0

**Income potential:** $500–$2,000/month

**What it is:** Tutor younger students or peers in subjects where you excel — math, science, reading, music, or test prep.

**Why it works:** Tutoring rates run $20–$60/hour. Parents urgently seek tutors for struggling children. A student excelling in AP subjects can charge $40–$75/hour for SAT prep.

---

### 16. YouTube Channel

**Startup cost:** $0–$300 (basic camera or use a smartphone)

**Income potential:** $500–$50,000+/year (highly variable)

**What it is:** Build a YouTube channel around a genuine passion — gaming, science experiments, cooking, art tutorials, or comedy.

**Why it works:** Young creators have authentic voices that audiences connect with. Channels with 100,000+ subscribers earn meaningful ad revenue. Brand partnerships follow for successful channels.

---

### 17. Reselling (Thrift Flipping)

**Startup cost:** $50–$200 (initial inventory)

**Income potential:** $200–$2,000+/month

**What it is:** Buy undervalued items at thrift stores, garage sales, or estate sales and resell on eBay, Depop, or Facebook Marketplace.

**Why it works:** Vintage clothing, toys, electronics, and collectibles bought for $2–$20 routinely sell for $30–$200+. Kids with good taste and research skills can build profitable reselling operations.

---

### 18. App Design / Coding

**Startup cost:** $0–$100 (learning resources)

**Income potential:** $500–$10,000+ per project

**What it is:** Build simple apps, websites, or games for clients or for app stores.

**Why it works:** A teenager who learns to code can freelance for local businesses needing simple websites at $500–$2,000 each. Build a useful app and the upside is uncapped.

---

### 19. Podcast

**Startup cost:** $50–$200 (basic USB mic)

**Income potential:** $500–$10,000+/year (ad-supported)

**What it is:** Host a podcast on a topic you're passionate about — gaming, pop culture, news, or educational content for kids.

**Why it works:** Niche podcasts with loyal audiences attract sponsorships. Teen-hosted podcasts aimed at teens have a natural authenticity advantage over adult-produced content.

---

### 20. Photography & Videography Services

**Startup cost:** $200–$1,000 (basic camera)

**Income potential:** $500–$3,000/month

**What it is:** Shoot photos and videos for local businesses, events, and social media.

**Why it works:** Small businesses need affordable content creators. A teen photographer can charge $100–$300 for a local business shoot — affordable for the client, excellent income for the kid.

## School-Based & Community Businesses

### 21. School Store / Snack Stand

**Startup cost:** $50–$200 (inventory)

**Income potential:** $100–$500/month

**What it is:** Run a small store or snack stand at school selling stationery, snacks, or novelty items.

**Why it works:** Captive audience, no rent, low competition. School stores run by student entrepreneurs teach purchasing, inventory, and cashflow in a controlled environment.

---

### 22. Event Planning for Kids' Parties

**Startup cost:** $50–$200 (supplies, decorations)

**Income potential:** $200–$800/event

**What it is:** Plan and coordinate kids' birthday parties — decorations, games, activities, and coordination.

**Why it works:** Parents value stress-free party planning. A creative teenager can charge $200–$500 to plan and run a birthday party while the parents enjoy themselves.

---

### 23. Homework Help Club

**Startup cost:** $0

**Income potential:** $200–$800/month

**What it is:** Run an after-school homework help group — either in-person or virtual — for younger students.

**Why it works:** Group tutoring at $15–$25/student per session with 5–10 students generates $75–$250 per session. Parents love structured after-school options.

---

### 24. Errand Running / Concierge for Neighbors

**Startup cost:** $0–$20 (bike, bags)

**Income potential:** $100–$500/month

**What it is:** Run errands for elderly neighbors or busy parents — grocery pickup, pharmacy runs, library returns, package pickup.

**Why it works:** Elderly community members value this service and pay generously for trustworthy local help. Charge $10–$30 per errand depending on complexity.

---

### 25. Recycling Collection Service

**Startup cost:** $0–$50 (wagon or cart)

**Income potential:** $50–$300/month

**What it is:** Collect recyclables from neighbors (cans, bottles) and take them to redemption centers.

**Why it works:** In states with bottle deposits, this teaches environmental responsibility and earns real cash. Scale to more houses for more income.

---

## Seasonal & Event-Based Businesses

### 26. Holiday Decor Installation & Removal

**Startup cost:** $0 (use clients' decorations)

**Income potential:** $200–$1,000/holiday season

**What it is:** Help neighbors put up and take down holiday lights and decorations.

**Why it works:** Many people want festive decorations but hate the hassle. Charge $50–$200 per house for installation and the same for removal. October–December and January are the peak windows.

---

### 27. Summer Camp Helper

**Startup cost:** $0

**Income potential:** $500–$2,000/summer

**What it is:** Assist local summer camps, sports programs, or recreation centers as a junior counselor or activity leader.

**Why it works:** Older teens (16+) can work as paid junior counselors. Younger teens can assist as volunteers while building experience for paid positions.

---

### 28. Holiday Card Writing Service

**Startup cost:** $10–$30 (stationery, stamps)

**Income potential:** $200–$600/holiday season

**What it is:** Offer calligraphy or handwritten holiday card services for busy parents and businesses.

**Why it works:** Beautiful handwriting is increasingly rare. Charge $3–$8 per card written and addressed. Elderly clients who want to send dozens of cards will happily pay.

## Unique & Underserved Ideas

### 29. Virtual Assistant for Older Adults

**Startup cost:** $0

**Income potential:** $200–$800/month

**What it is:** Help elderly neighbors or family friends with technology tasks — setting up phones, teaching video calling, organizing files, or managing email.

**Why it works:** Tech-savvy kids provide massive value to older adults who struggle with technology. Charge $15–$25/hour for patient tech support. Referrals are abundant.

---

### 30. Bike Repair Service

**Startup cost:** $30–$100 (basic tools)

**Income potential:** $100–$500/month

**What it is:** Repair and tune up bikes for neighbors — flat tires, brake adjustments, chain lubrication, gear tuning.

**Why it works:** Simple bike repairs that take 30 minutes can be charged at $20–$50. Tune-ups before spring season are in-demand. Watch YouTube tutorials and practice on your own bike first.

---

### 31. Book Review Blog

**Startup cost:** $5–$20/month (web hosting)

**Income potential:** $100–$1,000+/month (affiliate commissions)

**What it is:** Write book reviews and earn affiliate commissions when readers buy through your links.

**Why it works:** Amazon Associates pays 4–7% commission on books. A teen reader who publishes 200+ book reviews and gets Google traffic earns passive income while doing something they love.

---

### 32. Teaching Music Lessons

**Startup cost:** $0 (if you already play an instrument)

**Income potential:** $400–$1,500/month

**What it is:** Teach music lessons to younger kids in guitar, piano, violin, drums, or voice.

**Why it works:** Private music lessons run $30–$80/hour. A teen with 3+ years of instrument experience can teach beginners. 10 students at $40/week each = $400/week.

---

### 33. Art Lessons

**Startup cost:** $20–$100 (art supplies)

**Income potential:** $200–$1,000/month

**What it is:** Teach drawing, painting, or crafts to younger kids in small group or private lessons.

**Why it works:** Art classes run $25–$50/hour. Kids who excel at art can run weekend art clubs or after-school sessions. Parents happily pay for enrichment activities.

---

### 34. Scooter / Skateboard Repair

**Startup cost:** $30–$80 (basic tools and parts)

**Income potential:** $100–$400/month

**What it is:** Repair and upgrade scooters, skateboards, and longboards for peers and neighbors.

**Why it works:** These are common toys with repairable parts that most parents don't know how to fix. Charge $10–$40 per repair. Market in neighborhood Facebook groups and school networks.

---

### 35. Subscription Box Curation for Peers

**Startup cost:** $100–$300 (initial inventory)

**Income potential:** $200–$2,000/month

**What it is:** Curate monthly boxes themed around a niche — slime, art supplies, snacks, STEM activities — and sell subscriptions to other kids through parents.

**Why it works:** Kids understand what other kids want better than adult companies do. A well-curated $20/month box with 50 subscribers generates $1,000/month in recurring revenue.

## How Parents Can Support a Young Entrepreneur

1. **Start with a family business review** — discuss the idea, expected costs, pricing, and who the customers are
2. **Open a kid's bank account** — make income and expenses tangible
3. **Be a first customer and referral source** — share with your network
4. **Let them fail small** — better to lose $20 now than $200,000 later
5. **Celebrate effort over profit** — the skills being built matter more than the revenue

---

## The Bottom Line

Kids who run businesses grow into adults who think like entrepreneurs — resourceful, resilient, and able to create value where others see problems. The specific business matters far less than the act of starting.

**Pick one idea, spend less than $50, and take the first step this weekend.** The lessons learned from that first nervous conversation with a potential customer are worth more than any class on entrepreneurship.

The best time to start is childhood. The second best time is today.
