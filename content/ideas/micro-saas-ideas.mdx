The myth of the VC-backed SaaS startup — 18-month runway, a team of 12, $2M in seed funding before a single customer — is increasingly out of touch with how the best software businesses get built.

Micro SaaS is the alternative: small, focused tools built by one or two people that solve a specific problem for a specific audience. No investors. No office. No hiring process. Just product, distribution, and revenue.

The best micro SaaS businesses generate $10K–$100K MRR with a single founder. Here are 27 ideas ready to build in 2026.

---

## What Is Micro SaaS?

Micro SaaS has no official definition, but practitioners generally mean:

- **Small team:** 1–3 people, often solo
- **Narrow scope:** Does one thing exceptionally well
- **Small-to-medium market:** $1M–$100M TAM, not unicorn territory
- **High margins:** 70–90% gross margin like any SaaS
- **Profitable early:** Revenue covers costs within 6–12 months

The goal isn't a billion-dollar exit. The goal is a profitable, sustainable business that gives you leverage over your time.

---

## The Micro SaaS Opportunity in 2026

Three forces are creating more micro SaaS opportunities than ever before:

**1. AI reduced the build cost by 10x.** A solo founder with Cursor, Claude, and a few AI APIs can ship a product in weeks that would have taken a team 6 months in 2020.

**2. Distribution platforms matured.** AppSumo, Product Hunt, Twitter/X, and niche communities give individual founders access to thousands of potential customers without a marketing budget.

**3. Niche markets are now big enough.** The long tail of B2B workflows — each worth $50K–$5M/year to the right tool — has grown large enough to sustain solo businesses.

## Category 1: Workflow Automations

### 1. Automated Google Review Responses

**The problem:** Local businesses (restaurants, salons, doctors, contractors) need to respond to every Google review to maintain their ratings. It takes 5–10 minutes per response and most businesses skip it.

**Market size:** 35 million Google Business profiles in the US. 78% of business owners don't respond to reviews consistently.

**Build:** Connect to Google Business API. Use AI to draft personalized responses based on review sentiment and business category. Business owner approves in one click.

**Monetization:** $29–$79/month per location. 500 customers = $175K–$475K ARR. Very sticky — businesses that see their ratings improve won't cancel.

**Distribution:** Cold email + outreach to local service businesses. Partner with reputation management agencies.

### 2. Slack Standup Bot with AI Summaries

**The problem:** Engineering and product teams use Slack standup bots but end up with long, unread threads. Nobody knows who is blocked without reading every message.

**Market size:** Slack has 20 million daily users. The async collaboration tools market is $3.8 billion.

**Build:** Slack app that collects standup responses and generates a prioritized daily digest — highlighting blockers, cross-team dependencies, and velocity metrics.

**Monetization:** $5–$10/user/month. At 5,000 users, that's $300K–$600K ARR.

**Distribution:** Slack App Directory listing. Content marketing targeting "remote standup alternatives."

### 3. Email Signature Generator and Manager

**The problem:** Companies with 10–100 employees spend hours manually updating email signatures across the team when branding changes. There's no central control — everyone has their own signature, inconsistently formatted.

**Market size:** 4 billion email users. Email signature management is a $150M+ market with aging incumbents (Exclaimer, CodeTwo) that charge enterprise prices.

**Build:** Admin dashboard where a manager creates signature templates. Each employee generates their own signature and updates propagate company-wide instantly.

**Monetization:** $49–$149/month per company. 1,000 companies = $588K–$1.8M ARR.

**Distribution:** Partner with IT service providers and Microsoft 365/Google Workspace resellers.

### 4. Invoice Follow-Up Automation

**The problem:** Freelancers and small agencies chase late invoices manually — a degrading, time-consuming experience that strains client relationships. 35% of invoices are paid late.

**Market size:** $3 trillion in outstanding invoices at any given time globally. Even a sliver of the freelance/small business segment is enormous.

**Build:** Connect to QuickBooks, FreshBooks, or Wave. Monitor invoice due dates. Automatically send escalating follow-up sequences — first a friendly reminder, then firmer messages — and stop when paid.

**Monetization:** $19–$49/month. 2,000 customers = $456K–$1.2M ARR.

**Distribution:** Freelancer communities (Reddit, Indie Hackers, Twitter). Partner with accounting software through their app directories.

### 6. Meeting Cost Calculator and Time Blocker

**The problem:** Organizations hold too many unnecessary meetings because nobody tracks the true cost. A 10-person meeting with senior engineers can cost $2,000 per hour.

**Market size:** US companies waste an estimated $37 billion per year on unnecessary meetings. This is a wide-open space for awareness and productivity tooling.

## Category 3: Business Intelligence Micro-Tools

### 12. Churn Reason Analyzer

**The problem:** When SaaS customers cancel, most companies send a generic cancellation survey. 80% of people skip it or say "too expensive" — not the real reason. Founders make product decisions on bad data.

**Build:** Exit interview automation. When a subscription is cancelled, trigger a conversational AI interview via email or in-app. Extract themes and tag churn reasons automatically. Dashboard shows trending cancellation reasons over time.

**Monetization:** $99–$299/month. Any SaaS company losing $10K+/month to churn will find this invaluable.

**Distribution:** SaaS founder communities (Indie Hackers, MicroConf). Cold outreach to SaaS companies with visible churn.

## How to Pick Your Micro SaaS Idea

With 27 ideas on this list, the hardest part isn't finding an idea — it's committing to one.

### The Solo Founder Selection Framework

Ask three questions before committing:

**1. Can I reach 10 paying customers in 30 days?**
This is about distribution, not product. Do you have access to the target audience through your network, existing communities, or a clear cold outreach strategy? If you can't identify 10 potential customers today, rethink the idea.

**2. Can I build a working prototype in 2 weeks?**
Micro SaaS succeeds when founders ship fast and iterate. If the MVP requires 3 months of development, you're building a startup, not a micro SaaS. Use AI tools, no-code backends, and existing APIs to cut build time.

**3. Is there an existing market for this solution?**
Check Google search volume (even 500–1,000 searches/month is enough for a micro SaaS). Look for Reddit threads where people describe the problem. Find at least one competitor — it validates that people pay for solutions in this space.

### The Validation Minimum: 3 Pre-Sales

Before writing a single line of code, close 3 pre-sales. Not leads, not signups — actual money exchanged.

Build a landing page with a clear value proposition and pricing. Offer a "founding member" discount. Then find 3 people who will pay you today.

If you can't close 3 paying customers in 2 weeks, the idea may not be strong enough — or your positioning needs work. Both are valuable signals before you've spent 6 months building.
