## The Problem: Real Estate Due Diligence Hasn't Changed Since 1995

The US real estate investment market is dominated by two groups: large institutions with proprietary technology and data, and individual investors with spreadsheets and hustle. There are approximately **17 million individual real estate investors** in the US — people who own 1–20 investment properties. They collectively control **$4.7 trillion** in residential investment property.

These investors are flying blind.

### The Manual Analysis Stack

When an individual investor evaluates a property today, here's what actually happens:

**Step 1 — Find the property** (2–4 hrs/week, ongoing): Manually browse Zillow, Realtor, Redfin, local MLS, Facebook groups, and Craigslist. Miss 60% of opportunities because they're in "off-market" channels — wholesaler lists, estate sales, bank-owned properties — that require relationships to access.

**Step 2 — Build the pro forma** (2–4 hrs per property): Open a spreadsheet. Manually enter purchase price, estimated rehab, ARV (after-repair value), estimated rents, taxes (look up county assessor), insurance (call agent), vacancy rate (guess), management fees (call 2 property managers), maintenance reserves (guess). Run the numbers. Most investors use the same static spreadsheet they downloaded from a BiggerPockets forum in 2018.

**Step 3 — Validate rent estimates** (1–2 hrs per property): Look at Craigslist, Zillow Rent Zestimate, Rentometer. None of these sources are reliable for small markets — Zillow's rent estimates are notoriously inaccurate in secondary markets, and Rentometer covers only major metros.

**Step 4 — Pull comps** (1–2 hrs per property): Log into Redfin, manually search for recently sold properties within 0.5 miles, similar square footage, similar bedrooms. There's no good data on rental property sales vs. owner-occupant sales — the investor is using comps that may be priced by emotion rather than investment math.

**Step 5 — Assess condition** (1 hr, only if worth pursuing): Drive the property or look at photos. Estimate rehab from photos, which is essentially impossible.

**Total**: 7–13 hours of manual work per property seriously analyzed, with high error rates at every step. A good investor can evaluate 8–12 properties per month. An institutional investor with automated underwriting evaluates hundreds.

### The Cost of This Inefficiency

The real cost isn't time — it's **deal quality and deal flow**.

Individual investors systematically overpay because their rent estimates are wrong. They underbid because they over-estimate rehab costs using gut feel. They miss deals because they can't monitor every listing source simultaneously. And they're completely blind to the **off-market universe** — the 30–40% of investment properties that trade without ever hitting the MLS.

A 5% improvement in deal accuracy across a 10-property portfolio worth $3M means $150,000 in additional equity at purchase. That's what better data is worth.

## The Idea: Automated Deal Sourcing and Underwriting for Individual Real Estate Investors

**PropFlow** is an AI-powered platform that monitors property listings across 40+ data sources in real time, automatically underwrites every property against an investor's criteria, and delivers ranked deal alerts with full financial analysis in under 5 minutes.

### Module 1 — Universal Deal Sourcing

PropFlow aggregates listings from:

**On-market sources**: MLS feeds (via RETS/RESO Web API), Zillow, Realtor, Redfin, Homes.com, Auction.com, HUD home listings, and REO (bank-owned) property feeds from major servicers

**Off-market sources**: Wholesaler email lists (PropFlow integrates with 200+ wholesaler CRMs), probate court filings (scraped from county court records), pre-foreclosure notices (NODs, lis pendens), tax delinquent lists (pulled from county treasurer websites), and expired/withdrawn MLS listings

**For sale by owner**: Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, and FSBO.com scraped and deduplicated

This gives PropFlow visibility into **40–60% more inventory** than investors who only watch the MLS — including the highest-yield off-market properties that most individual investors never see.

**Alert system**: When a property matches an investor's saved criteria (market, property type, bedroom count, cap rate threshold, price range), PropFlow sends an immediate push notification with the full underwriting analysis already completed. The investor opens the alert, reviews the pre-built analysis, and decides whether to pursue — no manual work required.

### Module 2 — AI-Powered Underwriting

Every property PropFlow analyzes receives a full pro forma in 3–5 minutes:

**Automated Rent Estimate (ARE)**

PropFlow's rent model uses a proprietary training dataset: 28 million rental listing transactions across 450 US markets, updated weekly. The model inputs:
- Property characteristics (beds, baths, sqft, year built, garage, laundry type)
- Neighborhood characteristics (walkability, school ratings, crime index, commute to employment centers)
- Comparable active rentals (current competing listings within 0.5 miles)
- Comparable recent leases (what properties actually rented for in the last 90 days)
- Seasonal adjustments (rents in many markets are 8–12% higher in summer than winter)

**Output**: A point estimate rent with confidence interval. "3BR/2BA in this zip code: $1,650–$1,790/mo, best estimate $1,720. 87% confidence based on 34 recent comps." This is materially better than Zillow Rent Zestimate, which doesn't weight recent comps or current competing inventory.

**Automated Financial Analysis**

PropFlow pulls and populates every line of the investment analysis automatically:

| Line Item | Data Source |
|-----------|-------------|
| Purchase price | Listing data |
| Estimated ARV | Comp sales analysis (AVM) |
| Gross rents | PropFlow ARE model |
| Property taxes | County assessor API |
| Insurance estimate | National insurance rate database by property type + zipcode |
| HOA fees | Listing data |
| Management fees | Local market management fee database (PropFlow surveys 2,000+ PMs) |
| Vacancy rate | Local market vacancy data (HUD, CoStar, PropFlow model) |
| Maintenance reserves | 5–15% of rent, adjusted by property age and type |
| Rehab estimate | Condition assessment from listing photos (computer vision model) + age-based estimates |

**Output metrics**: Cap rate, cash-on-cash return, gross rent multiplier, net operating income, debt service coverage ratio (at current rates), monthly cash flow, and a 10-year projected hold analysis with appreciation assumptions.

**The rehab estimate AI**: PropFlow's computer vision model analyzes listing photos and assigns a condition score (A/B/C/D) based on observable conditions: flooring type and visible wear, kitchen and bath finish level, windows, HVAC equipment age (visible in photos), roof condition. This converts to a rehab cost estimate. Not perfect — but calibrated: beta testing shows ±18% accuracy vs. actual contractor bids, which is significantly better than an investor's gut estimate at ±40%.

### Module 3 — Portfolio Intelligence

Once an investor is buying deals through PropFlow, the platform becomes their portfolio management layer:

**Active monitoring**: For each property owned, PropFlow watches local market rents monthly and alerts when market rent has moved enough to justify re-leasing at a higher rate ("Market rents in your Columbus zip code increased $85/month since you signed your current lease 14 months ago — you're $75/month below market").

**Refinance alerts**: When rates move or a property has appreciated enough to support a cash-out refi at favorable terms, PropFlow calculates and alerts.

**Comparable sale alerts**: When a comparable property in your market trades, PropFlow tells you what it means for your property value.
